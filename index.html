My Ideal Webpage.

Complicated Doesn't Mean Good

We mock the garishness of the animated GIFs that dominated the early web, and yet we see pop-ups for privacy settings as acceptable and somehow treat auto-playing videos as if they are anything other than a crime against humanity.

Simplicity Benefits the User

People come to a page to read the specific content of that page. Nearly all additions to the page beyond that make the life of the visitors worse because it is explicitly making the purpose of their visit harder.

Does a sidebar filled with trending articles make it easier or harder to focus on the article the visitor came to the site for? The pop-ups pleading for the user's email address whenever their mouse moves away from the page are not for the users.

Complexity Obscures Self-Interest

The complexity of websites exists solely to subvert the interests of the visitor and exploit them for the benefit of the site owner.

The more complex a page is, the more tools they are employing to ensnare the user. It becomes less of a world wide web, and more of a spider's web -- with each site trying to maximize the minutes the user spends on their site knowing full well that is not in the user's interest.

A Focus on Purpose

Not every site needs to be complex and filled with features! So much of what I enjoy on the web is text-based and I find even many of the stock photos interspersed to be annoying.

Unless it's directly related to the article, it's not there to help me with an article I'm already enjoying. It's there to keep me visually engaged in an article that I'm not enjoying and might otherwise stop reading.

Let's Keep It Simple.

This page doesn't use JavaScript! Why? Because there's absolutely no need for it! The page is simple and the content is the focus. There are a million use cases for JavaScript obviously, but this page doesn't need it. A lot of them don't.

It is fully accessible for screen readers and loads quickly on any device. The simplicity of this stylesheet would bend your bones! This should be the goal of most websites. And yet, many pages have conflated complexity with quality.

The entirety of this site's CSS:

body {
    font-family: Arial, sans-serif;
    background-color: #eee;
    color: #333;
    margin: 0 auto;
    max-width: 600px;
    line-height: 1.5;
    font-size: 1.15rem;
}

In Summary

The web is a great tool for communication and sharing information! But so many of its modern design principles exist to cover up that many of these sites are not very good at their core purpose, so they need to employ all manner of techniques to obscure that and keep visitors locked in to content they might otherwise find lacking.
